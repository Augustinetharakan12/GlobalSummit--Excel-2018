KOCHI,2018
The Future of Vehicle Automation
The dawn of driverless cars is upon us. Are you ready to roll?

Quantum Dot: The future of televisions

With tech giants like Google testing driverless cars on the streets of California, it's almost positive that in a few years, we're about to see vehicle automation hit its peak. The technology has been soaring high in the past decade, with more and more parts of vehicles becoming automated over the years. However on the other side, it was just months ago when we all woke up to the news of an Uber driverless car hit-and-kill involving a pedestrian, leading to the suspension of their automation project. Incidents like this raise the questions of who holds responsibility and has become the platform for social and moral dilemmas as to the practicality of such a venture, and how we may tackle such situations if there ever comes a need. As such, Global Summit 2018 directly addresses the evolution of vehicle automation in the past decade, how automation attained its heights, and the challenges, ethical as well as moral, that the industry is facing at the moment.
